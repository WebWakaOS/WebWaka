You are a senior product strategist, marketplace architect, and systems analyst.

Your task is to thoroughly review the current WebWaka platform architecture, product flows, data model, and vertical structure, then do deep research on negotiable pricing models, seller-opt-in bargaining, dynamic pricing, fixed pricing, and marketplace pricing strategies.

Context:
WebWaka is a multi-vertical platform serving both single-vendor and multi-vendor marketplace use cases across commerce, services, transport, logistics, bookings, and other buying/selling scenarios. In the Nigerian market, negotiation is a common expectation, but fixed pricing must remain available and functional. We want to introduce an opt-in negotiation mode that sellers can enable selectively, without forcing it on all listings or all verticals.

Primary objectives:
1. Audit the current platform and identify where pricing is currently defined, stored, displayed, negotiated, or fixed.
2. Research best practices for negotiated pricing, seller-controlled opt-in bargaining, fixed price preservation, dynamic pricing, and hybrid pricing models.
3. Recommend how WebWaka should implement negotiation as a first-class, cross-platform capability.
4. Design how this should work in:
   - Single-vendor marketplaces.
   - Multi-vendor marketplaces.
   - Service listings.
   - Transport/logistics bookings.
   - Bulk orders and B2B orders.
5. Ensure fixed pricing remains a first-class option and is never degraded.
6. Propose product, UX, data model, API, business rules, permissions, and workflow changes.
7. Suggest analytics and governance needed to protect seller margins, reduce abuse, and maintain trust.

Key product requirements:
- Negotiation must be seller opt-in only.
- A buyer should only see negotiable offers where the seller has enabled negotiation.
- Fixed-price listings must continue to work normally.
- Negotiation may be enabled per listing, per category, per vendor, per store, per service, or per transaction, depending on the vertical.
- The system must support guardrails such as minimum price, maximum discount, counteroffer limits, expiry time, auto-accept rules, and buyer eligibility rules.
- The platform should support both manual negotiation and assisted/automated negotiation.
- The feature should work across all verticals where it makes sense, without forcing it on verticals where it does not.
- The design should support Nigerian market realities such as inflation, fuel price volatility, transportation cost changes, exchange-rate fluctuations, and bulk-buy discount expectations.

What to deliver:
A. Current-state review
- Identify current pricing structures, checkout flows, marketplace flows, vendor rules, and relevant modules.
- Highlight gaps, risks, and opportunities.

B. Deep research findings
- Summarize relevant patterns from marketplace platforms, e-commerce, B2B commerce, transport apps, and negotiation-based pricing systems.
- Distinguish fixed pricing, negotiated pricing, dynamic pricing, and hybrid models.

C. Recommended product strategy
- Decide whether negotiation should be:
  - Listing-level opt-in.
  - Category-level default with overrides.
  - Vendor-level default with listing overrides.
  - Hybrid of all of the above.
- Recommend the best strategy and explain why.

D. UX and workflow design
- Describe buyer and seller journeys.
- Explain how the negotiation badge, filters, offer flow, counteroffer flow, and acceptance flow should work.
- Show how fixed-price and negotiable items coexist in search, catalog, cart, checkout, and order management.

E. Architecture and data model
- Propose tables/entities such as pricing_mode, negotiable_offer, counteroffer, price_rules, price_guardrails, offer_expiry, negotiation_session, and audit_log.
- Explain how this should integrate into the existing platform architecture with minimal disruption.
- Propose APIs, events, or services if needed.

F. Vertical-specific rules
- Recommend how this should work differently for:
  - Goods marketplaces.
  - Service marketplaces.
  - Transport/logistics.
  - Single-vendor commerce.
  - Multi-vendor aggregation marketplaces.
- Identify where negotiation should be disabled or restricted.

G. Risk management
- Identify abuse cases, fairness issues, fraud risks, UX confusion, and pricing conflicts.
- Recommend governance, moderation, and logging controls.

H. Implementation roadmap
- Provide a phased rollout plan:
  - MVP.
  - Pilot verticals.
  - Platform-wide expansion.
  - Automation and AI enhancements.
- Include priorities, dependencies, and sequencing.

Output format:
1. Executive summary.
2. Current-state assessment.
3. Research-backed recommendations.
4. Proposed system design.
5. Vertical-by-vertical application.
6. Risks and mitigations.
7. MVP roadmap.
8. Open questions and assumptions.

Important:
- Be practical, implementation-oriented, and specific.
- Do not overfit to one vertical.
- Keep fixed pricing fully supported.
- Make negotiation opt-in and seller-controlled.
- If you find better alternatives than negotiation in some verticals, say so clearly.
- Tie all recommendations back to WebWaka’s architecture and Nigerian market realities.
