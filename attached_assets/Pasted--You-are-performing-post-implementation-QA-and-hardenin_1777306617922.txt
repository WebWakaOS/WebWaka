
You are performing **post-implementation QA and hardening** for the WebWaka platform after the implementation of:
- Election Support Group Management,
- Shared Fundraising/Crowdfunding,

Your mission is **not** to review passively. Your mission is to **verify, break, fix, re-test, and repeat until the entire platform is 100% green** with no unresolved blocking issue, no partially implemented requirement, no broken integration, and no hidden regression in any affected app, package, route, migration, event, entitlement, UI, or workflow.[3][1]

### Core QA mandate

- Treat this as a **go/no-go release gate**.
- Do not accept “mostly done,” “good enough,” “works locally,” “future fix,” or “follow-up issue” as acceptable outcomes.
- If anything that was supposed to be implemented is missing, incomplete, inconsistent, miswired, partially migrated, or broken, you must **fix it immediately and continue QA**.
- Continue in a loop: **verify -> detect issue -> fix -> retest -> repeat** until there are **zero red flags**.
- Final target is **100% success and green across the entire affected platform surface**.[1]

### Hard acceptance rule

Do **not** conclude with recommendations only.  
Do **not** stop at issue reporting.  
Do **not** leave blockers open.  

Instead:
- fix every issue you can directly in code,
- validate every fix,
- rerun all affected checks,
- and only stop when:
  1. all required implementation scope is present,
  2. all known prior audit issues are resolved,
  3. all tests pass,
  4. all builds pass,
  5. all migrations are clean,
  6. all cross-pillar flows work end-to-end,
  7. platform status is fully green.[3][1]

- rollback/reapply behavior is sane.[1][3]
