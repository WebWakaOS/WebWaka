## Core Principle

Political roles must be modeled as structured assignments over territories, not as loose labels attached to people.

## Political Office Types

| Office | Territory Scope |
|---|---|
| Councilor | Ward |
| Local Government Chairman | LGA |
| State House of Assembly Member | State Constituency |
| House of Representatives Member | Federal Constituency |
| Senator | Senatorial District / Zone |
| Governor | State |
| President | Country |

## Territory Types

- Ward
- LGA
- State Constituency
- Federal Constituency
- Senatorial District / Zone
- State
- Country

## Political Objects

| Object | Description |
|---|---|
| Political Office Type | The defined role type (e.g. Senator) |
| Political Territory Type | The geographic scope of authority |
| Jurisdiction | A specific territory instance |
| Political Assignment | A person's active holding of an office over a jurisdiction |
| Party Affiliation | Association between an Individual and a political party |
| Candidate Record | Pre-election representation for a person seeking office |
| Term Record | The start/end window of a confirmed assignment |

## Examples

- A Councilor is assigned over a Ward.
- A Senator is assigned over a Senatorial District.
- A House of Representatives Member is assigned over a Federal Constituency.
